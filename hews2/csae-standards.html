Definitions

  • CSAE: Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation, including grooming, solicitation, trafficking, sextortion, or any other behavior that sexually exploits or endangers minors.
  • CSAM: Child Sexual Abuse Material, including any imagery, video, text, or links that depict or sexualize minors.
  • Actual knowledge: When Hews 2 for Hacker News becomes aware of CSAE content through user reports or moderator review.

Prohibited Content & Conduct

Through Hews 2 for Hacker News, users must not:

  • Access, share, promote, or attempt to distribute CSAE or CSAM content.
  • Use Hews 2 for Hacker News to groom, solicit, or contact minors for sexual purposes.
  • Share links, images, or discussions that sexualize or exploit minors.

If such use is detected, Hews 2 for Hacker News will restrict access and, where required by law, report the matter to relevant authorities.

Detection & Moderation

  • Hews 2 for Hacker News does not host Hacker News content, but may take steps to limit visibility of harmful material in the client.
  • If CSAE is reported via email, Hews 2 for Hacker News will:
    • Review the report,
    • Escalate concerns to Hacker News administrators (Y Combinator), and
    • Report to law enforcement or child protection agencies as required by law.
